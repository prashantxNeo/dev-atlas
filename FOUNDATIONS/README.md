# Foundation

>This section marks the beginning of my journey as a programmer.

Now that I look back at it, it feels like I was learning the core basic skills that every beginner should learn, but it wasn't like this in the beginning.

When I started out, I was pretty much clueless about computer programming, what I really wanted to accomplish, or what exactly I should learn. I watched countless roadmap videos, read articles, browsed Reddit and Discord and what not, trying to figure out where to begin.

### The Odin Project & MDN Documentation

> But somehow, someday I landed on "The Odin Project (TOP)."

Firstly, I was so skeptical, but then I read the curriculum, how the course works, the Odin community, the syllabus, the founder notes, and stories from people who learned from The Odin Project. Everything was in one place with almost literally no loose ends.

For the first time, I felt like I had landed somewhere I should begin my journey, somewhere I didn't have to run into the chaos of thousands of concepts in an unorganized way. Soon enough, MDN Documentation also came to the rescue whenever I felt weak on certain concepts. Since then, these two have been my most important resources for learning computer programming.

#### Beauty of The Odin Project & MDN Documentation

One of the most confusing things about learning a new skill is that you'll get introduced to a topic, and inside that topic there are many smaller concepts you have no idea about. As a beginner, you never know whether you should learn them now, later, or much later. Literally no idea at all.

One of the most amazing things about TOP & MDN Docs is that they simply tell you:

> "You may have noticed this new attribute. We'll study it in the near future, no need to worry about."

A nice pat on the shoulder. Simply beautiful, like an awesome teacher.
